## Social Media Do’s 
• Audit Your Digital Shadow: Search your own name in an "Incognito" window once a semester to see what a recruiter sees.

• Showcase Your Projects: Post about a club event you organized, a coding project you finished, or a volunteer shift you completed.

• Network Strategically: Follow industry leaders and comment thoughtfully on their posts to build professional visibility.

• Use High-Quality Images: Use a clear, friendly headshot for your LinkedIn and professional profiles to build immediate trust.

• Fact-Check Before Sharing: Verify news or academic claims before hitting "repost" to ensure you aren't spreading misinformation.

## Social Media Don’ts 
• Don't Post "Vent" Content: Avoid complaining about your professors, difficult exams, or your current internship; it signals a lack of professional discretion.
 
• Don't Overshare Your Schedule: Posting exactly where you are in real-time can pose a physical safety risk.

• Don't Forget the "Delete" Isn't Permanent: Assume everything you post—even in "private" groups—can be screenshotted and shared publicly.

• Don't Neglect Your Bio: Leaving your bio blank or using unprofessional language makes your profile look like a "bot" or an abandoned account.

• Don't Ignore Privacy Settings: Never assume a platform’s default settings are private. Manually check them every time an app updates.
