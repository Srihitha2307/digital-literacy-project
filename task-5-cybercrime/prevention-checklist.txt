Stay Safe Online: Prevention Checklist

General Digital Hygiene

Enable Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA): Use app-based authenticators for your university ID, email, and social media.

Update Software Regularly: Keep your phone’s OS and apps updated to patch security holes.

Use Strong, Unique Passwords: Use a password manager; never use your birthdate or "123456" for multiple accounts.

Be Skeptical of "Urgent" Messages: If a WhatsApp message claims your account is blocked or offers a "dream job," it is likely a scam.

Secure Your Social Media: Set profiles to private to prevent identity theft and cyberbullying.

Avoid Public Wi-Fi for Sensitive Tasks: Never log into bank accounts or student portals using canteen or station Wi-Fi.

Financial & UPI Safety

 PIN is for Sending, Not Receiving: You never need to enter your UPI PIN to receive money. If someone asks for a PIN for a "refund," it is a fraud attempt.

 Verify Payee Name: Always double-check the verified name on your UPI app (GPay/PhonePe) before hitting 'Pay.'

How to Report

If you are a victim of cybercrime, act immediately:

National Helpline: Call 1930 (Available 24/7).

Online Portal: Report at cybercrime.gov.in.
